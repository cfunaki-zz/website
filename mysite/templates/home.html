Let me introduce myself

Hi there, I'm a student at the University of Southern California with a passion for learning. My friends know me as the soccer guy and data guru, and that sums up my ambitions pretty well: to be a data scientist or data engineer on the forefront of sports analytics.

At heart I'm a kid full of curiosity, looking for new experiences to grow and learn from. I live for adventure, whether that means going to unfamiliar places, eating bizarre foods, or falling from the sky. Below are just a few snapshots of my adventures during college.

I'm constantly pushing myself to learn more and do more. Even when I'm not learning, I enjoy helping others learn. If I'm not busy being a student, you can find me tutoring economics to USC's student athletes, giving workshops about data analysis to members of the Applied Statistics Club, or coaching kids from USC's surrounding area through Trojan Youth Soccer League.

My most recent project was my NBA shot chart tool which allows you to visualize the shooting of any player or team in the NBA in the last few years. You can see how efficiently a player or team is shooting from any given location on the court and where they take most of their shots from.

Feel free to check out my blog as well. Here, you can find my other projects and ideas that I think are worth sharing.
